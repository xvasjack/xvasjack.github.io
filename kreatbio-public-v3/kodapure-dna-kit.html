Workflow

Magnetic-bead capture from lysis through clean elution.

01

Lysis

5-10 min

Cells and tissue are lysed so DNA is released into solution.

02

Bead binding

10-15 min

Magnetic beads capture DNA while contaminants remain in the liquid phase.

03

Magnetic separation + wash

20-25 min

A magnet pulls the bead-bound DNA to the tube wall while wash buffers clear carryover.

04

Elute

10 min

Elution buffer releases purified DNA into a clean tube for downstream use.

FAQ

Questions buyers usually ask before a KodaPure trial or quote.

Does KodaPure support FFPE tissue?

FFPE should be reviewed case by case during technical evaluation. The current public workflow is positioned around fresh or frozen tissue, whole blood, buffy coat, and cultured cells.

What inputs are covered in the public review range?

Representative public ranges cover tissue up to 25 mg, whole blood up to 200 uL, buffy coat, and cultured cells up to 5 x 10^6.

Can the workflow be scaled for higher-throughput screening?

The public workflow is a manual tube-based method. Higher-throughput planning should be reviewed during procurement so sample volume, staffing, and document needs are clear up front.

Can KodaPure DNA be used for bisulfite or methylation workflows?

Methylation and other specialized downstream workflows should be reviewed after sample QC because fragment quality and cleanup targets matter.
