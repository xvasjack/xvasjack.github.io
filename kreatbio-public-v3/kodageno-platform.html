Lesson experience

How KodaGeno teaches.

KodaGeno does not just show commands. It asks the learner what to do next, shows why that choice matters, and moves step by step through the analysis flow.

  • Guided choices instead of a flat worksheet
  • Reasoning attached to each step so the learner understands the choice
  • Short lesson blocks that fit classroom, cohort, or self-study use

Who it is for

Three common ways people use KodaGeno.

This is the plain version: classroom use, team rollout, or direct learner practice.

Academic programs Use KodaGeno when a course needs guided sequencing analysis practice.

Departments can give students a clearer first step than static slide decks or command lists.

Institutions and teams Use KodaGeno when a group needs setup, launch, and reporting.

This is the path for cohorts, instructor access, learner invitations, and progress review.

Individual learners Use KodaGeno when one learner wants guided practice before live project work.

The live lesson library lets a learner start without waiting for a team rollout.

Team setup

What team rollout looks like on KodaGeno.

This section is where team setup now lives.

01
Planning Agree the learner group, timing, and module scope.

Start with seat count, target learner level, module selection, and delivery dates.

02
Onboarding Set up instructor access and learner invitations.

Prepare the cohort list, instructor logins, and the first learner message before launch.

03
Launch Open the cohort and start the first lessons.

Teams move from setup into live use with the agreed lesson path and timing.

04
Reporting Review completion, activity, and next-step recommendations.

Use the reporting cycle to check learner progress and decide what comes next.

Reporting view

What a team reviews after launch.

  • Lesson completion by cohort
  • Activity level by learner group
  • Module progress for instructors and program leads
  • Next-step recommendations for the next block of lessons
