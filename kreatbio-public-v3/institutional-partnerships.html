Design phase

What is defined before the program starts.

  • Learner level: undergraduate, postgraduate, professional, or mixed cohort
  • Program format: semester course, bootcamp, workshop, or annual access
  • Module scope and lesson order
  • Reporting schedule and assessment needs
Onboarding phase

What happens before learners are invited.

  • Seat list and cohort setup
  • Instructor account access
  • Learner invitation and activation timing
  • First module release and launch communication
Launch phase

What a first rollout looks like.

  • Pilot cohort before a larger rollout
  • Learner invitation checks and access confirmation
  • Initial lesson release and instructor orientation
  • Launch timing built around the academic calendar
Reporting phase

What institutions can review during the program.

  • Module completion and lesson progress
  • Cohort-level activity summary
  • Instructor follow-up and next-term planning
  • Renewal or expansion discussion
Institution-only benefits

What institutions get that direct learners do not.

  • Instructor-facing progress dashboard
  • Cohort-level completion and activity review
  • Launch-page and learner-facing communication options
  • Dedicated program contact during the agreement term
  • Priority access to new module releases during active program terms
Typical program formats

Three common ways institutions deploy KodaGeno.

  • University semester course: 14 weeks, 1-2 modules per week, instructor-led delivery
  • Intensive bootcamp: 5-10 days, concentrated module stack, post-course completion review
  • Continuous access program: annual seat agreement with self-paced learners and instructor oversight
Universities

Department-led teaching

Built for departments and centers that want sequencing analysis training inside semester teaching or structured lab programs.

Training providers

Academy or bootcamp delivery

Useful for private academies and specialist training groups that want guided bioinformatics lessons without building the curriculum from scratch.

Professional programs

Continuing education

Suitable for workforce upskilling, lab-transition training, and modular genomics education outside a full degree program.

Program terms

What an institution is actually buying when it deploys KodaGeno.

Partnership discussions move faster when the delivery model, pricing variables, and expected handoff materials are stated clearly.

Pricing basis

Programs are quoted against learner seat count, term length, branding scope, reporting needs, and whether KodaPure kits are bundled into the teaching plan.

Agreement shape

Delivery can be scoped as a single cohort, a bootcamp-style program, or annual institutional access depending on how learners are scheduled.

Partner deliverables

Institutions review seat planning, instructor access, learner invitation flow, onboarding materials, and reporting cadence before launch is approved.

What to bring to the first review

Come with learner numbers, term length, delivery format, branding needs, and whether wet-lab kit support is part of the program discussion.

Delivery outputs

Concrete materials institutions should expect during setup and reporting.

Institutions should be able to see what gets set up, what gets reported, and how the reporting cycle works.

Seat setup list Learner count, instructor access, cohort format, and start date.

These are the minimum operational details needed before launch is approved.

Onboarding checklist Seat confirmation, lesson access, instructor orientation, and learner invitation.

The onboarding phase should end with a clear launch checklist, not just a verbal handoff.

Report sample Completion rate, module progress, learner activity, and next report date.

Institution buyers should see the reporting shape before they commit to a term plan.

Reporting cycle Launch report, mid-term progress update, and end-of-term summary.

That gives departments a visible reporting rhythm instead of a vague promise of oversight.

Commercial terms

Institutional partnership economics are available by agreement.

Pricing usually depends on learner seat count, program length, branding scope, reporting needs, and whether KodaPure kits are bundled into the deployment plan.
